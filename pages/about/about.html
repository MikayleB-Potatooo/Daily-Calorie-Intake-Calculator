How does the daily calorie intake calculator work?

The calorie calculator is based on the Mifflin - St Jeor equation. It allows you to find your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) - the amount of energy needed for your body to support only its basic vital functions. It differs for men and women.

BMR(men) = 10 * weight(kg) + 6.25 * height(cm) – 5 * age(y) + 5

BMR(women) = 10 * weight(kg) + 6.25 * height(cm) – 5 * age(y) – 161

After you calculate your BMR, you need to multiply it by a factor corresponding to your physical activity level:

1. Sedentary lifestyle (little or no exercise): 1.2

2. Slightly active lifestyle (light exercise or sports 1-2 days/week): 1.4

3. Moderately active lifestyle (moderate exercise or sports 2-3 days/week): 1.6

4. Very active lifestyle (hard exercise or sports 4-5 days/week): 1.75

5. Extra active lifestyle (very hard exercise, physical job or sports 6-7 days/week): 2.0

6. Professional athlete: 2.3

How do I use the daily calorie intake calculator?

To use the calculator app, start first by clicking the calculator app tab found at the bottom of the page. Afterwards, you may now begin to input the necessary details and information needed to calculate your calorie intake such as gender, height in cm, weight in kg, age, and activity level. Once you have filled up the necessary details, you may now click on the calculate botton in order to view your needed daily calorie intake.
